How do we use yellow
1. Yellow = Action
Use yellow to require user actions
e Buttons
e Availability indicators
e "Available today" tags
This helps train users that:
Yellow = "click me" or "pay attention"

2. Yellow = Safety and Trust
Use yellow to display information
e Safety notices
e Trailer rules
e License requirements
e Weight limits
An example would be:
e Small yellow line or icon next to:
e Max load
e Road-legal

3. Yellow = Navigation (don't make it
obvious)
Instead of a yellow background:
e Thin yellow underline on active menu
item
e Yellow hover state (mainly used on
buttons)
e Yellow progress steps in "how it works"

This feels like the yellow reflectors on the
road - guiding


Layout format:
e Dark background = night road
e White content block = road signs
e Yellow accents = reflectors guiding
movement

Gives the impression that you are driving
and being guided to moving forward.

Micro-interactions
e Buttons glow slightly yellow on hover

e Step numbers in yellow circles

Typography
Avoid playful fonts - trailer =
responsibility.
e Headings: Bold, strong, no curves
e Body text: Simple, readable
e Use yellow behind text highlights, not
on text itself (accessibility)

Core neutrals (foundation)

Off-white (background)
#F8F9FA
Clean, soft, modern (easy on eyes)

Light grey (sections / cards)
#E9ECEF
Perfect for separating sections without heavy lines

Dark charcoal (text / headings)
#212529
Strong contrast, more premium than pure black

🟡 Accent & signal colors (use with intention)

Yellow (reflector / primary accent)
#FFC107
High visibility, energetic, familiar (safety color)

Blue (trust / info / secondary actions)
#0D6EFD
Calm, reliable, professional

Red (warnings / errors only)
#DC3545
Clear danger / alert signal — don’t overuse

Orange (attention / highlights)
#FD7E14
Softer than red, good for “important but not dangerous”

How I’d personally assign them (Subject to change)

Off-white → main background
Light grey → cards, sections, footers
Dark charcoal → ALL text
Yellow → primary CTA, availability, steps
Blue → links, info messages
Orange → promos, “limited availability”
Red → form errors, warnings only

If you misuse red or yellow — you break trust.
Use them sparingly and they feel powerful.
