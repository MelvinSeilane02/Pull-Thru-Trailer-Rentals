Naming formula (simple & scalable)
[Size] + [Purpose] + (Optional weight hint)

This way:

- Users instantly understand
- You avoid confusion\
- It scales when you add more trailers

🚛 Small trailers (light loads)

750kg or less | short length
- Compact Utility Trailer
    - Best for small moves, boxes, light furniture
- Garden Load Trailer
    - Ideal for garden waste, rubble bags
- Mini Haul Trailer
    - Everyday personal use

Why this works:
- “Compact / Mini” = no intimidation
- Feels beginner-friendly

🚚 Medium trailers (most popular)

750kg – 1,200kg | medium length
- Standard Utility Trailer
    - Your bread & butter
- Furniture Mover Trailer
    - Instantly relatable
- Business Load Trailer
    - Appeals to hustlers & small businesses
These should dominate your Top Picks section.

🚛 Large trailers (heavy-duty)

1,200kg+ | long / reinforced
- Heavy Load Flatbed
- Contractor Trailer
- Max Load Trailer

These names signal:
- Strength
- Serious work
- Not for casual users

🏍️ Specialized trailers (optional, later)

- Bike Transport Trailer
- Car Transport Trailer
- Closed Cargo Trailer
- Flatbed Equipment Trailer

Only add these when demand exists.

🟡 Pro UX tip (important)

On the card:

- Show the name
- Then a small line under it:
    - “Up to 750kg”
    - “Heavy-duty – 1.5 ton”

Users don’t want maths — they want reassurance.

❌ Names to avoid

- Model numbers only (T750-XR)
- Internal codes
- Funny names
- Too many technical terms

If someone needs to ask “what is this?”, the name failed.
